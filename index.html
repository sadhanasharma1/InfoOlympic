Achievements

Cricket Players

Sachin Tendulkar

Widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in cricket history, Tendulkar holds numerous records, including being the highest run-scorer in both Test and One-Day Internationals (ODIs). He was the first to reach 100 international centuries and played a key role in India's 2011 World Cup triumph.

Mahendra Singh Dhoni

MS Dhoni, the iconic Indian cricket captain, achieved tremendous success during his tenure. He led India to victories in the ICC T20 World Cup in 2007, the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011, and the ICC Champions Trophy in 2013. His calm and strategic leadership, along with his exceptional skills as a wicketkeeper-batsman, earned him immense respect and admiration worldwide. Dhoni's achievements have left an enduring legacy in Indian cricket history.

Virat Kohli

Known for his aggressive batting and exceptional consistency, Kohli has amassed a remarkable number of runs in all formats of the game. He became the fastest to score 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 11,000 ODI runs and has led India to several series victories as captain.

Rahul Dravid

Nicknamed "The Wall," Dravid was known for his solid technique and dependable batting. He scored over 10,000 runs in both Tests and ODIs and played pivotal roles in India's victories abroad, including series wins in England and Australia.

Kapil Dev

An iconic all-rounder, Kapil Dev captained India to its first-ever World Cup win in 1983. He was an explosive batsman and a fast bowler with over 400 Test wickets, making him a trailblazer for future generations of Indian cricket.

Anil Kumble

One of the finest leg-spinners in cricket history, Kumble is India's leading wicket-taker in Tests. He achieved the rare feat of taking all ten wickets in an innings against Pakistan in 1999 and played a vital role in India's Test successes during his illustrious career.

Football Players

Sunil Chhetri

India's all-time leading goal scorer with over 100 international goals, Sunil Chhetri's lethal attacking prowess has led India to SAFF Championships. As Bengaluru FC's captain, he played a vital role in winning the ISL title, earning numerous individual accolades, and inspiring the nation with his leadership and goal-scoring ability.

Gurpreet Singh Sandhu

Breaking barriers as the first Indian goalkeeper in the UEFA Europa League, Gurpreet Singh Sandhu's shot-stopping skills have been crucial for the Indian national team. He played a vital role in India's Intercontinental Cup win and has earned recognition as AIFF Player of the Year.

Sandesh Jhingan

India's defensive stalwart, Sandesh Jhingan impressed during the 2019 AFC Asian Cup. Named AIFF Emerging Player of the Year, he moved to Croatia's top division, showcasing his potential on the global stage.

Anirudh Thapa

A creative midfield maestro, Anirudh Thapa played a crucial role in India's Intercontinental Cup win and contributed significantly to Chennaiyin FC's ISL triumph.

Udanta Singh

Known for his electrifying pace and mesmerizing dribbling skills, Udanta Singh has been a key player for Bengaluru FC and a valuable asset for the Indian national team.

Hockey Players

Dhyan Chand

Widely regarded as one of the greatest hockey players of all time, Dhyan Chand led India to three Olympic gold medals in 1928, 1932, and 1936. His incredible goal-scoring abilities earned him the nickname "The Wizard of Hockey."

Balbir Singh Sr

A legendary forward, Balbir Singh Sr. was part of the Indian team that won three Olympic gold medals in 1948, 1952, and 1956. He holds the record for the most goals scored in an Olympic final, netting five in the 1952 final against the Netherlands.

Dhanraj Pillay

One of India's most skilled and charismatic players, Pillay's achievements include leading India to victory in the 1998 Asian Games and the 2003 Asia Cup. He also represented India in four Olympic Games and several World Cups.

Javelin Throw Players

Neeraj Chopra

Neeraj Chopra is India's top javelin thrower and won the gold medal at the 2018 Asian Games. He also represented India at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics, where he won the historic gold medal with a throw of 87.58 meters, becoming the first Indian to win an Olympic gold in athletics.

Annu Rani

Annu Rani is a prominent Indian javelin thrower and holds the national record in the event. She won the silver medal at the 2014 Asian Games and has represented India in multiple international competitions.

Shivpal Singh

Shivpal Singh is another talented javelin thrower from India. He has represented the country in various international events, including the Asian Games and World Championships.

Davinder Singh Kang

Davinder Singh Kang is known for his javelin throwing abilities and has competed in the Olympics and other international tournaments, representing India.

Rajinder Singh Rahelu

Rajinder Singh Rahelu is a seasoned javelin thrower who has represented India at various international events, including the Asian Games and Commonwealth Games.

Marathon

Shivnath Singh

Shivnath Singh was one of India's early marathon stars. He represented India in the 1976 Montreal Olympics and finished 11th in the marathon, setting a national record that stood for over four decades.

Asha Agarwal

Asha Agarwal is a renowned Indian marathon runner who won the gold medal in the 1981 Asian Marathon Championships held in Japan. She was one of the pioneering female distance runners from India.

Sunita Godara

Sunita Godara is a prominent Indian marathon runner who won the gold medal in the women's marathon at the 1992 Asian Marathon Championships. She also represented India in the 1992 Barcelona Olympics.

Gopi T. (Thonakal Gopi)

Gopi Thonakal became the first Indian male athlete to win the Asian Marathon Championship when he won the gold medal in the 2017 edition held in China. He also represented India in the 2016 Rio Olympics.

Sudha Singh

Sudha Singh is a notable Indian distance runner who won the gold medal in the women's 3000m steeplechase at the 2010 Asian Games. While she is primarily a steeplechase specialist, she has also competed in marathons and represented India in various international events.

Venue

Narendra Modi Stadium

The Narendra Modi Stadium in Motera, Ahmedabad, Gujarat, India, is the world's largest cricket stadium with a seating capacity of around 132,000 spectators. Formerly known as Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel Stadium, it was renamed in February 2021 to honor India's Prime Minister, Narendra Modi. The stadium's modern design offers a 360-degree unobstructed view of the playing field, making it an exceptional venue for international and domestic cricket matches. With state-of-the-art facilities and multiple practice grounds, it stands as a remarkable symbol of India's love for cricket and its significance in the sporting world.

Vivekananda Yuba Bharti Krirangan

Vivekananda Yuba Bharati Krirangan, commonly known as Salt Lake Stadium, is a multipurpose stadium located in Kolkata, West Bengal, India. It is the second-largest stadium in the world by seating capacity, with a capacity of approximately 85,000 spectators. The stadium has hosted numerous sporting events, including football matches, athletics competitions, and concerts. It is the home ground of several football clubs in Kolkata and has witnessed several historic football matches. The stadium's vast size and modern facilities make it a significant landmark in India's sports infrastructure, attracting sports enthusiasts and spectators alike from across the country.

Eden Gardens

Eden Gardens is an iconic cricket stadium situated in Kolkata, West Bengal, India. It is one of the oldest and most prestigious cricket grounds in the world, known for its rich history and passionate cricket fans. With a seating capacity of around 66,000 spectators, Eden Gardens has hosted numerous historic cricket matches, including World Cup finals and test matches. It is the home ground of the Bengal cricket team and the Kolkata Knight Riders in the Indian Premier League (IPL). The stadium's electric atmosphere and cricketing heritage make it a beloved venue for players and fans alike.

Shaheed Veer Narayan Singh International Cricket Stadium

Shaheed Veer Narayan Singh International Cricket Stadium, located in Naya Raipur, Chhattisgarh, India, is a modern cricket stadium known for its state-of-the-art facilities and beautiful architecture. It is named in honor of Shaheed Veer Narayan Singh, a prominent freedom fighter from Chhattisgarh. The stadium has a seating capacity of approximately 65,000 spectators and serves as a venue for domestic and international cricket matches. With excellent amenities and a picturesque setting, it has become a significant sporting landmark in the state, attracting cricket enthusiasts and players alike.

Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium

The Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium, commonly known as Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium or JLN Stadium, is a multipurpose sports stadium located in New Delhi, India. Named in memory of India's first Prime Minister, Jawaharlal Nehru, the stadium has played a crucial role in hosting various sporting events over the years. With a seating capacity of around 60,000 spectators, it has been the venue for major sporting events, including international football matches, athletics competitions, and the opening and closing ceremonies of the 2010 Commonwealth Games. Its significant contributions to sports and its central location in the capital city make it a prominent and cherished stadium in the country.

Greenfield International Stadium

Greenfield International Stadium also known as The Sports Hub and previously as Trivandrum International Stadium is a multi-purpose stadium in Kerala, India used mainly for cricket.[3] The stadium is located at Kariavattom in Thiruvananthapuram city. It was built on 36 acres of land leased by the Kerala University for ₹94 lakh (US$146,527.23) per year for a period of 15 years.
